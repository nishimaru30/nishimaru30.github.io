Projects

A few things I've built or investigated along the way — some ongoing, some wrapped up before the PhD.

Ongoing · PhD

Chiral moieties & stereoselective catalysis

Representing chiral molecules computationally and building machine learning models that can tell stereoselective reactions apart — the mirror-image chemistry where a small structural flip changes everything about how a reaction behaves.

Aganitha

Reaction modeling

Studied cross-electrophile coupling reactions to work out what drives their mechanisms and pathways, feeding directly into better reaction conditions and product selectivity. Implemented DFT-B to speed up otherwise expensive DFT jobs without giving up much accuracy.

Aganitha

Force field parameterization for small molecules

Built classical force fields (GAFF, tailor-made force fields) for accurate molecular dynamics simulations, then explored machine-learned potentials — specifically Torch-MD Net — to push accuracy and efficiency further.

Aganitha

Solubility prediction

Designed molecular descriptors from DFT-B calculations and paired them with statistical and machine learning models to predict solubility — accurate enough to be useful, interpretable enough to guide the next experiment.

Aganitha

Reaction yield prediction

Built and compared machine learning models to predict and classify yield on Buchwald–Hartwig HTE data, curating descriptors that made sense to bench chemists, not just to a model.

Aganitha

Protein–ligand modeling for drug discovery

Docking (AutoDock, DiffDock), molecular dynamics on PROTAC systems, and xTB-based binding energy estimates — giving medicinal chemists a computational head start before anything reaches the bench.
