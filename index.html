About Me

I’m Nishi Maru, a computational chemist working at the intersection of quantum chemistry, cheminformatics, and machine learning. My research focuses on building data-driven models that connect molecular structure to reactivity, selectivity, and function.

I am currently a Marie Skłodowska-Curie PhD Fellow at the University of Groningen, where I work on data-driven modeling of stereoselective catalysis as part of the CATALOOP doctoral network, under the supervision of Dr. Robert Pollice.

Previously, I worked at Aganitha Cognitive Solutions on early-stage drug discovery, predictive analytics, and high-throughput molecular modeling. I also gained teaching and academic experience at the University of Utah.

Outside research, I enjoy writing, storytelling, and exploring how AI, chemistry, and creativity intersect through long-form blogging and science communication.
